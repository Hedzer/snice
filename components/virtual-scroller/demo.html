Performance Stats

The virtual scroller only renders items that are visible in the viewport, plus a small buffer. This means that even with thousands of items, only a few dozen are actually in the DOM at any time, resulting in excellent performance.

Without virtual scrolling: Rendering 10,000 items would create 10,000 DOM elements, causing slow initial render and sluggish scrolling.

With virtual scrolling: Only ~20-30 items are rendered at any time (depending on viewport height and item height), with smooth 60fps scrolling.
